Founder

Ian Swift Hunter

Founder & GM, Anemoia
Former Senior Vice President of A&R, Atlantic Records

Ian Swift Hunter is the founder of Anemoia, a New York-based artist development company, launched in 2023 as an experimental imprint of Atlantic Records.

Early Career & Warner Music Group

Hunter spent nearly 15 years working within the Warner Music Group system, getting his foot in the door as a Physical Analyst at WEA. Big Beat had relaunched as Atlantic's electronic imprint and was unknowingly sending release promos to a music blog Hunter had been running since college. Big Beat met Hunter, launching his A&R career as a Remix Commissioner. His unique skillset garnered the attention of Atlantic CEO Craig Kallman and then President of Worldwide A&R Mike Caren — Hunter became Warner Music Group's first-ever dedicated A&R Research executive, a first at any major label.

Alongside Caren, Hunter pitched the concept of using data for artist discovery, presenting to Warner labels in the U.S., Germany, France, and UK. This period included discovering a then-unknown Melanie Martinez — one of the earliest proofs that data could surface culturally significant artists. After early successes including a global breakthrough from Norwegian producer Matoma ("Old Thing Back"), Hunter was recruited by Craig Kallman to co-found Atlantic's A&R Research department.

Atlantic Records Tenure

Over the next decade, Hunter helped architect Atlantic's discovery infrastructure — including exclusive partnerships with Shazam, Sodatone, Microsoft, and Y Combinator — while evolving into a prolific creative A&R. His rare dual fluency in data and creative instincts produced a roster spanning pop, street rap, alternative, country, electronic, hard rock, and Christian music.

Hunter delivered RIAA Gold and Platinum debut albums across genres — a rarity in the modern era — with Melanie Martinez, Why Don't We, Breland, JayDaYoungan, Matoma, and Sueco. He also played a pivotal role in relaunching Asylum and East West in the U.S., securing their first modern-era RIAA certifications.

The Launch of Anemoia

At the end of 2022, Ian was offered the opportunity to steward his own label under Atlantic. Anemoia launched with a vision for a new Artist Development model. Notable early signings included:

The Hellp (Complex Artist to Watch 2025; Coachella DoLab; sold-out US/EU tour) · Amira Elfeky (Spotify Artist to Watch 2024; sold-out US tour; opener for Bring Me The Horizon EU tour) · Southstar ("Wild Ones" / "Cover Up My Face," EU charting techno producer)

Before departing Atlantic, Ian celebrated a run of wins including Oliver Tree & Robin Schulz – "Miss You" (1B+ streams; multi-platinum globally; Brit Awards 2024 nominee), Billy Gillies ft. Hannah Boleyn – "DNA (Loving You)" (BPI Platinum; UK Top 10), and COBRAH – Succubus (Swedish Grammis 2024 Best Electronic/Dance Album).

Speaking & Industry Roles

Guest lecturer at Stanford University's CCRMA (2017–2019). Featured speaker at SXSW 2018 "The Future of A&R." Panelist at by:Larm festival, Oslo. GRAMMY Dance/Electronic Awards Committee, 2019.

Today

Hunter has since acquired the Anemoia trademark back from Warner Music Group and relaunched the company independently. He also founded Anemoia Songs, the company's publishing arm.
